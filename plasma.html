Importance of Plasma Donation

What is Plasma?

Plasma is the liquid component of your blood, making up about 55% of the total blood volume. It contains water, electrolytes, antibodies, and proteins, including clotting factors.

How Does Plasma Donation Help?

Plasma donation is critical for various medical treatments and therapies. Donated plasma is used to create life-saving medications for patients with clotting disorders, immune deficiencies, and other medical conditions.

Life-Saving Treatments

Plasma-derived medications are used to treat individuals with hemophilia, immune system disorders, burn patients, and more. They help prevent bleeding, fight infections, and save lives.

COVID-19 Pandemic

During the COVID-19 pandemic, convalescent plasma (plasma from recovered COVID-19 patients) played a crucial role in treating severe cases. Plasma donation can make a significant impact during health crises.
